## Overview
This living watchlist covers private or emerging companies developing optical I/O, CPO/NPO engines, silicon photonics, novel emitters, packaging, coupling, and adjacent photonic-compute or manufacturing technology. Funding values use announced rounds rather than private-database estimates; “known funding” is therefore a floor.

The list separates active core optical-networking companies from adjacent companies, public companies, acquired companies, and unresolved names. Funding size does not establish revenue, production qualification, customer adoption, or manufacturing readiness.

> ⚠️ Note: Private valuations, customers, foundry agreements, and cash runway are often confidential. Ambiguous names require a legal entity or canonical URL before being treated as market participants.

### Core Company Profiles
**Ayar Labs**
- Technology: TeraPHY optical-I/O chiplet and SuperNova external laser.
- Differentiator: dense WDM optical I/O designed for in-package integration.
- Target: AI/HPC silicon and systems vendors.
- Risks: package qualification, yield, customer concentration, HVM timing.
- M&A Candidate: MAYBE; semiconductor, foundry, or AI-platform acquirer.

**Lightmatter**
- Technology: Passage photonic interconnect with wafer-scale/3D integration concepts.
- Differentiator: high-density scale-up fabric; photonic-compute heritage.
- Target: AI accelerator and system platforms.
- Risks: manufacturing scale, architecture adoption, customer qualification.
- M&A Candidate: MAYBE; AI silicon or systems vendor.

**Ranovus**
- Technology: Odin optical engines and ORON CPO platform.
- Differentiator: multi-terabit SiPh and quantum-dot-laser integration claims.
- Target: switch and AI systems.
- Risks: commercialization cycle and public HVM evidence.
- M&A Candidate: YES; networking or optical-semiconductor vendor.

**Nubis Communications**
- Technology: compact low-power linear optical engines.
- Differentiator: high-density engine architecture.
- Target: AI and networking systems.
- Risks: qualification, scale, and incumbent competition.
- M&A Candidate: YES; optical component or switching vendor.

**Avicena**
- Technology: LightBundle microLED-based short-reach interconnect.
- Differentiator: massively parallel visible-light emitters targeting low energy.
- Target: chip-to-chip and memory fabrics.
- Risks: new emitter ecosystem, packaging, reliability.
- M&A Candidate: MAYBE; memory, packaging, or compute vendor.

**OpenLight**
- Technology: foundry-accessible SiPh platform with integrated InP lasers.
- Differentiator: heterogeneous laser integration through an open platform.
- Target: datacom, telecom, sensing, and compute.
- Risks: PDK adoption, customer tapeouts, manufacturing economics.
- M&A Candidate: YES; EDA, foundry, or optical-semiconductor vendor.
